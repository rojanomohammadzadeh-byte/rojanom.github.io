Artist Statement

My practice moves between sculpture, installation, and material-based research, exploring migration, liminality, and processes of transformation. I am interested in how bodies and materials carry memory, and how displacement reshapes both physical and emotional structures.

Through fragile and often unstable materials, I construct forms that reflect states of transition—between places, identities, and languages. These works function as temporary architectures of experience, where breakdown, glitch, and fragmentation become visual languages rather than failures.

My work is grounded in personal and collective narratives of displacement, especially within the context of political and cultural rupture. I use material tension to mirror psychological states of uncertainty, adaptation, and survival.
